I am not in stars, nor darkness, nor fury's dissonant roar,
not in the chime swayed by southern winds.

I dwelled in stone. I was the fleeting ancient stag,
the terror in a predator's gaze,
the sculpted ring of fire unyielding to oblivion.
You will not find me in this dry grassland,
you will not find me in the hollow of this earth;
for I breach death again and again.
Surpassing death, I enter the flowing current of history,
and time after time, crossing beyond myself,
I fuse with that very history.
I seep into you, into your kin, into the warp of your being.
Scorching heat, storm, monsoon—
I am woven into your very being.
Not I, but you, are my reflection.
Beyond the ages of stone, forest, and the fleeting stag,
I press forward, tirelessly drawn to you.
The woods are not mine, nor the river, nor stars.
I gave you the forest's longing, the river's ceaseless flow.
The flower, the bird, the moonlight—not mine—
yet I gave you the festival of flowers and birds,
and I gifted you the moonlight's sorrow.
Lifelong companion, I am in your dreams, your battles.
Because I stand with you, applause thunders at the music hall;
because I stand before you,
the sealed gates of celebration tremble.
