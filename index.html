Chapter 01 · Preface

From Barishal to Stockholm,
by way of curiosity.

I am a person built from broken pieces, most at home adrift in a current of my own. I sleep, argue with the other half of myself, and write things no one needs to read; I teach machines to dream, but only sparingly. My only wish is never to live on anyone else's time.

In short

Now

Data Engineer (AI) at SciLifeLab Data Centre, building platform tools and AI services for life-science researchers.

Always

It's okay to pause, to reflect, to drift a little against the current: no more rat race, no more borrowed goals, only a little laughter and a little sunshine from my Ongshi.

Chapter 05 · Doctoral work

Teaching machines to notice illness.

Detecting COVID-19, and the first hours of sepsis. Care shaped around the person, devices that listen in the background, and models that learn and explain together without ever sharing what's private, , on messy real-world data.

Doctoral Degree Conferment Ceremony – Stockholm University, September 27, 2024, at Stockholm City Hall
The thesis

Advancing clinical decision support using machine learning & the Internet of Medical Things: Enhancing COVID-19 & early sepsis detection

Catching COVID-19, and the quiet early onset of sepsis. A case for healthcare that keeps the patient at the center and invites them into it: connected medical devices drawing scattered health data together, and methods that range from random forests and XGBoost to interpretable deep and federated learning.

Machine learning & healthcare

SHAMSUL

Chest X-ray · Pathology prediction . Interpretability

Systematic Holistic Analysis to investigate Medical Significance Utilizing Local interpretability methods in deep learning. The SHAMSUL Demo App explores interpretability for chest X-ray pathology predictions using methods—Grad-CAM, LIME, SHAP, and LRP. It provides heatmaps and evaluation metrics for better insights into the medical significance of predictions made by deep learning models.
